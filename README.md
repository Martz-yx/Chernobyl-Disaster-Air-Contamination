# The Chernobyl Disaster Air Contamination in neighbouring countries

The Chernobyl disaster was a nuclear accident that occurred on 26 April 1986 at the No. 4 nuclear reactor in the Chernobyl Nuclear Power Plant, near the city of Pripyat in the north of the Ukrainian SSR. It is considered the worst nuclear disaster in history and is one of only two nuclear energy disasters rated at seven - the maximum severity - on the International Nuclear Event Scale, the other being the 2011 Fukushima Daiichi nuclear disaster in Japan.
<br>
<br>The immense release of **radioactive material** from the explosion and subsequent fires was carried by **air currents**, resulting in widespread contamination that extended far beyond the immediate vicinity of the plant. This report specifically focuses on the **transboundary impact** of the contamination. We will analyze the **effects of the accident on neighboring countries** by examining how the radioactive plume traveled and deposited contaminants across international borders. Due to the atmospheric spread, the contamination was not limited to neighboring nations but was also **detected in countries significantly far away from the Chernobyl site**. This fact underscores the global scale of the disaster's initial airborne impact.
<br>
<br>This Report presents concentration of **Iodine-131 (I-131)**, **Caesium-134 (Cs-134)&#32;** and **Caesium 137 (CS-137)&#32;** as aerosol particles which were measured in specific location and date, providing concrete evidence of the disaster's reach and the resulting **environmental and public health concerns** in those affected nations.
